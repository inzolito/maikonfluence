Generic Haul Workflow

Upon login in you will be greeted with the production screen asking you to select a time allocation:

Please refer to the TIME documentation for how this bar works.

To continue with Hauling workflow you will need to select the ready state.

You will now see the hauling view.

You will notice the Taurus on the right hand side:

This wheel represents the different activity's the haul truck can be in throughout a haul cycle or shift.

The wheel can be hidden and shown for a better view of the map behind.

These options are configurable through silver light however, Empty and Loading are mandatory sections as these track required metrics.

Tapping on one of these sections will activate it if possible. You will notice int the default configuration tapping empty works however others require you to be empty before beginning.


The colors of the selected regions are also configurable from silver light.

The section on the left hand side is a summary of the information for this hauling cycle. As we are still in empty There is no use full information there currently.

Tapping the icon in the top right of the summary will cause it to expand into the form where this information can be updated.

Â 

It is not necessary to maximize this form as transitioning to the queued activity will open it up. This is because in the default workflow its assumed that while queued to be loaded is the best time for the worker to fill out this form.

Transitioning to Loading will also maximize the form to attempt to ensure the worker fills out the form. You will also not be able to transition to the hauling activity unless the form has been filled out to the minimum requirements.

Once the form is filled out and you successfully transition to hauling. The form will be minimized to give a better view of the map in the background. You will also recieve a hint reminding you that it is better to have pre-filled the destination you are heading to.

It should be noted. That you can move backward and forward on the wheel also skipping steps in certain areas. Tapping the cell directly next to the currently selected section will move to only in that direction if possible.

However being in Hauling for example and tapping Queued will move you backwards through the loading step, Not completing a cycle through empty. Inverting that situation moving from empty to hauling will pass through Loading if the form has been filled properly.

Moving from Hauling to Empty will complete a Haul cycle. This will affect your shift summary updating the number of completed hauls. It will also minimize the hauling form as it is assumed you are once again driving and view of the map would be more advantageous.

This summary can be expanded by tapping the button in the top right corner. This will give you a more detailed shift summary. Tapping the summary again will minimize it.


VIMS Intergration

VIMS integration status is visualized by the truck icon in the top right hand side of the title bar.

When this Icon is read, no response is being heard from any VIMS integrations.

When the Icon is green we are successfully receiving information from VIMS Integrations.


If the Haul truck is in Queued activity and receives the dipper signal the truck will transition into the loading activity.

When in the loading activity if the haul truck starts to move at speed the activity will transition to hauling.


Bluetooth Beacon integration

If the App has the NearbyDeviceMonitor functionality enabled. And there are beacons set up in the system.

While in queued or loading if a beacon is picked up that is tied to a location that is part of source locations determined by the haul query. It will populate the Source field with that location.

With Auto Populate destination enabled. The destination is no longer a required field before transitioning to hauling. However has become a mandatory field before transitioning to the empty activity.

When Transitioning to Hauling the source field will be locked as you and the beacons should no longer be setting the source.

While in hauling if a beacon is picked up that is tied to a location that is part of the destination locations determined by the haul query. It will auto populate the destination field with that location.


GeoFences

When Locations have been correctly Configured as GeoFences they will appear on the map like the following.

Entering one of these zones via GPS will trigger events.


If the geofence is configured to a source location while in queued or loading it will set the haul form properties to its properties
