LessonNotesAction

Need to have proper validation of software updates prior to deployment

Unvalidated patches cause the hyper care to be prolonged due to the constant cycle of fixing issues and causing other issues.

V1000s were delivered under extenuating circumstances and is a once off event.

Insufficient testing for V1000 hardware. PD do significantly more testing on the new display X hardware which is being field trialled

Develop more realistic environment for better stress testing

Consider fleet size and duration to deploy updates to a large fleet

Results in clashes between software release

Causes confusion


Create a hyper care standard to work with customer to nominate x% of equipment dedicated to field trialling patches.


Facilitates change management


Timing of initiation of hyper care

Typically initiated too late. Usually when customer makes a threat

Introduce criteria for hyper care initiation???

Time a critical case is in hypercare (e.g. 26 weeks)

Waste time deciphering what an issue is or working on the wrong issue


Request for videos (preferred), screenshots for tickets in hyper care

Additional training for support team to request for more details


Need for BU travel to site/timing of BU travel to site

Part of it is a PR exercise especially when the travel is late in the hyper care

Expedite communications and resolution

Introduce structure to BU travel:

  • Send GT first and/or application engineer
  • Standardized plan for BU at site (expedited change management, detailed schedule an plan of issues to be addressed)
    • Additional remote support from PD (afterhours if required)

Improve coordination amongst teams


Assign single point of contact in each team

Re-use some of the process to log and report individual issues.
