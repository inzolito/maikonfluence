Onboarding

Even though we still don't have a process established for this yet, every new person who comes to the team will receive an introduction about our hardware, what to add to a ticket they are working on, how to commit the work, how to test what they are doing in one of the hardware, and other details about everyday work. The team makes sure the new people feel more comfortable with the work and get enough understanding to start. Also, when planning the work, we allocate some time to have the new person onboarded by working together with another team member. So if you are new to the team, don't feel afraid to ask for help.


Estimates

The process for estimates involves:

  • Having enough understanding of a ticket before bringing it to an estimation session.
  • After we have enough information, the team will most likely use one of the Backlog Refinement meetings to discuss and estimate. If they can't agree, they usually ask for more information from the PM/PO or the ticket reporter.
  • We have an artificial sprint called 'RFR (Ready for Refinement)' to allocate the tickets that need to be discussed and estimated. After this, they are moved to 'RFL (Ready for Lifecycle)' meaning the ticket is ready to be done.
  • The team uses a tool to get everyone's estimate (we used to use this, but now it seems they are requiring registering to use https://planningpokerweb.com/ / https://planningpokeronline.com/).


Jira Board

Following alphabetical order, each team member chooses an animation, movie, or TV show to name the sprint.

The columns we have on our board are:

  • To do: the open work planned to be done during the sprint.
  • Blocked: tickets if we have any kind of blocker preventing the team from moving forward we move to this column (e.g. waiting for external feedback, dependency with other tickets).
  • In progress: work in progress.
  • Ready for QA: tickets that completed the development part and it was previously tested by the developers (before moving the ticket to QA, the developers update the development part of the checklist inside of the ticket, and leave it unassigned).
  • In QA: checks if the ticket has all the information needed from developers to start testing (e.g. build version, device, etc), and tests the ticket to see if it's working and if matches with what is asked in the description.
  • Done: tickets the team completed.


Scope change

Our goal is to bring value to customers and other users who use what we build. So when is needed to change the scope, we guarantee that is necessary by talking to our PM/PO to make sure the team is not over-committing. Before bringing a new ticket to the sprint, the ticket needs to be discussed and estimated. After that, we can swap the ticket with another one that is inside of the sprint and it has the same amount or more story points.
